Explore our collections

Women Working, 1800-1930

Women Working, 1800-1930

An exploration of women's impact on the economic life of the United States between 1800 and the Great Depression

Print and Photograph Collections

Print and Photograph Collections

The Fine Arts Library's print and photograph collections document works of art, architecture, and material culture from the ancient world to the present day.

Charlie Archive

Charlie Archive

Documenting the vigorous debate about fundamental political and ethical issues that followed the 2015 terrorist attacks on the French satirical magazine Charlie Hebdo.

Science Fiction Collection

Science Fiction Collection

Dating from the 1880s to the present, the Science Fiction Collection at Houghton Library includes trade paperbacks, magazines, fanzines, and prozines, as well as many works of science fiction, fantasy, and horror.

Images of Colonialism

Images of Colonialism

These late-19th- and early-20th-century trade cards and illustrations offer insight into European perspectives on the colonial experience.

Studies in Scarlet: Marriage and Sexuality in the U.S. & U.K., 1815-1914

Studies in Scarlet: Marriage and Sexuality in the U.S. & U.K., 1815-1914

Drawn from the Harvard Law School Library’s extensive trial collections, Studies in Scarlet features over 420 trial narratives involving the crimes and passions of both renowned and ordinary people.

Yiddish Empire: The Vilna Troupe, Jewish Theater, and the Art of Itinerancy
